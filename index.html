About Me

I am currently working as a Postdoctoral Researcher with Prof. Ki-Young Choi at Sungkyunkwan University in the Theoretical Astroparticle Physics and Cosmology(TAPPC) lab. I received my doctorate in Theoretical Particle Physics (November 2022) from the Indian Institute of Technology Hyderabad, where I worked with Prof. Narendra Sahu.

Research Interests

My research focuses on exploring physics beyond the Standard Model, with particular emphasis on neutrino physics, dark matter phenomenology, and matter-antimatter asymmetry in the early Universe. I am passionate about addressing key cosmological challenges, including the Hubble and S-8 tensions, and investigating intriguing phenomena such as gravitational waves and primordial black holes. Additionally, I am interested in anomalies like the muon (g-2) anomaly, which suggest new physics beyond the Standard Model. My work aims to develop comprehensive theoretical frameworks that unify multiple phenomena, contributing to our understanding of fundamental physics and the evolution of the Universe.

Recent Publications
Reconciling cosmological tensions with inelastic dark matter and dark radiation in a U(1)D framework
Wonsub Cho, Ki-Young Choi, Satyabrata Mahapatra
JCAP 09 (2024) 065
We propose a novel and comprehensive particle physics framework named 'SIDR+z_t' (Self Interacting Dark Radiation with transition redshift), based on an inelastic dark matter (IDM) scenario coupled with dark radiation, governed by a dark gauge symmetry, that addresses multiple cosmological tensions observed in recent measurements of the Hubble parameter, S8, and Lyman-α forest data.
Gravitational wave sourced by decay of massive particle from primordial black hole evaporation
Ki-Young Choi, Erdenebulgan Lkhagvadorj, Satyabrata Mahapatra
JCAP 07 (2024) 064
In this article, we investigate the stochastic gravitational waves (GWs) spectrum, resulting from the emission of gravitons through bremsstrahlung, in the decay of particles produced by Hawking radiation. In addition, we also discuss the stochastic gravitational waves constituted out of the gravitons directly radiated from such evaporating PBHs.
